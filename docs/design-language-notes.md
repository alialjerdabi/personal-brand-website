## Typography

- Voice: Schibsted Grotesk (variable) for all reading text and display. System register: Geist Mono for meta/data only (eyebrows, numbering, status line, client tags). Rule: *if a machine could have written it, it's mono; if Ali is saying it, it's Schibsted.*
- Display tiers observed: hero h1 `text-6xl/8xl/9xl`, tracking −0.04em · act headlines (turn, Act III heading) `text-4xl → 6xl`, tracking −0.03em · chapter outcomes `text-3xl/4xl`, tracking −0.02em. Tracking tightens as scale grows.
- Eyebrow pattern (recurring 3×): mono, 11px, uppercase, tracking 0.3em, muted ink.
- Statements use `font-semibold` (600); mid-level content `font-medium` (500); body 400. No other weights.
- Reading measures: body `max-w-md/xl`, statements `max-w-3xl/4xl`.

## Spacing

- Act padding: `py-28 sm:py-40` (Acts II–III). Hero is tighter at top by design (chrome + immediate headline).
- Intra-act rhythm: eyebrow → heading `mt-6`, heading → paragraph `mt-8`, block → block `mt-16/20/24`, chapter gap `my-16 lg:my-20` (handoff), major transitions `mt-24 sm:mt-32`.
- Plate framing (recurring 2×): bordered frame with `p-3 sm:p-4` around a 4/3 media well — hero preview card and Act III plates share it. Light ground: `border-zinc-200 bg-white`; dark ground: `border-white/10 bg-white/[0.02]`.

## Motion

- Micro (hover/active states): `transition-colors/opacity`, default duration.
- Content cross-fade (lens preview): 300ms.
- Entrance reveals: 400ms ease-out, fade + 1.5rem rise, only for below-fold elements; server default is always visible.
- The signature (revised 2026-07-20, Ali's direction): Act II is SCROLL-SCRUBBED again — a pure function of scroll position, not a timer (the 2026-07-19 timed version outran/underran visitors because Hero+SystemThread ate viewport before the section was reached). Mechanism: nothing plays until the heading crosses a "comfortable reading" anchor line (heading.top == 60% of viewport height); from there, a further scroll window drives progress 0→1 (no runway/pin needed — just the heading's own continuous position). Milestones as fractions of that 0→1 budget: 4 fragments at step .13/span .10 each (mask-rise, translate 110%→0%, easeOutCubic — NO CSS transition, the eased value is computed fresh every rAF frame from live scroll delta so it can never lag/outrun); each fragment recedes to zinc-500 (RGB-interpolated, continuous) exactly as the NEXT one starts rising, staying ink until superseded; PAYOFF_GAP=.16 (deliberately > the ~.03 inter-fragment idle gap) before the two-line takeaway (staggered .04 apart); bridge follows at +.05 gap. Initial state applied in useLayoutEffect (pre-paint, computed from real mount-time scroll position — works correctly for deep links, no flash, no "is it below the fold" heuristic needed). SSR/no-JS/reduced-motion: fully revealed, fragments ink (motion is progressive-enhancement only). Verified via DOM sampling: forward pass and a reverse pass landed on IDENTICAL intermediate values (bit-for-bit), proving true path-independence.
- BUG FIXED same day (2026-07-20, Ali caught it — the bridge line was still animating in as it scrolled past the top of the screen): the scroll window was originally a flat WINDOW_VH=1.5 (viewport-heights), completely decoupled from the block's real rendered height. Measured on real content: block height (heading→bridge) was 742–774px against a required 1080px window — the sequence needed MORE scroll to finish than the content had room to be on-screen for. Verified via DOM sampling (heading/li/turn/bridge offsetTop, cross-referenced against the exact formula from source): the bridge only reached full reveal at 81px of margin before scrolling off-screen entirely — under one trackpad flick. FIX: windowPx is no longer a viewport multiple — it's now `(bridge.offsetTop − heading.offsetTop) + (startLine − settleLine)`, i.e. the block's OWN measured height (offsetTop is layout-only, stable under the mask's translate) plus the viewport distance from the trigger line (60%) down to a new SETTLE_AT_VIEWPORT_FRACTION=0.3 anchor. This guarantees progress=1 lands with the bridge sitting at 30% down the viewport — comfortable reading room — on ANY content length or viewport height, not just the one screen size it happened to be tuned against. Re-verified same methodology post-fix: margin before exit grew from 19px to 216px at 1440×720 desktop, 244px at 375×812 mobile. Lesson: a scroll-scrubbed window's SIZE must be derived from the content it's revealing, never an arbitrary constant — the two can drift apart the moment content length or viewport height changes, and the failure is silent (no error, just a payoff that finishes off-screen).
- Superseded 2026-07-19 timed version (kept for history, see below) — the paced-timer approach; DEPRECATED, do not reintroduce: a fixed-duration autoplay-on-entry cannot satisfy "never outrun the visitor" once upstream sections (hero, sticky thread) vary how much viewport they consume before the section is reached.
- Vertical rhythm (2026-07-20): Problem section's bottom padding intentionally asymmetric vs its top (pb-32/48 vs pt-14/20) — this is the "absorb the idea" pause after the payoff. Capabilities' top padding intentionally tighter than its bottom (pt-20/24 vs pb-28/36) — momentum resumes immediately into its own heading rather than doubling the gap. Turn-line margin bumped mt-24/32 → mt-28/36 to echo the motion pause typographically even for reduced-motion/no-JS readers. Hero/SystemThread spacing (the L-cut) intentionally untouched — already tuned tight for continuous momentum. Rule: spacing decisions are per-seam and asymmetric (top ≠ bottom of the same section), never a global padding bump.
- Vertical rhythm, remaining seams (2026-07-20, same rule applied page-wide): Capabilities pb-16/20 + Philosophy pt-24/32 = compression INTO the dark climax; Philosophy pb-32/48 + Method pt-28/40 = the page's LARGEST breath (the exhale after the argument); Method pb-20/24 + Contact pt-20/28 = momentum carried into the ask; Contact pb-16/20 + footer pt-12/14, both hairlines REMOVED = one composed close (the page ends once). Divider rule refined: ground changes are the dividers; hairline dividers now exist only WITHIN acts (method rail, capability list), never between them.
- OLD (superseded) paced timed-reveal notes, retained for context only: Once the block enters view (IntersectionObserver, rootMargin -18%), fragments rise one at a time from behind a mask (overflow-hidden li + inner span translate 110%→0, 600ms) with STEP 900ms; each new line = ink (#09090b), prior lines fade to LEGIBLE mid-gray (#71717a / zinc-500, NOT near-white — must stay readable at display size). After a longer pause (1350ms) the two turn lines land as the takeaway (ink, semibold, 140ms internal stagger); bridge follows quiet. Ref-driven (direct DOM writes, no re-renders); pre-paint useLayoutEffect masks the lines so there's never a flash; SSR/no-JS/reduced-motion = all lines visible, fragments ink. (History: scroll-scrubbed scatter→converge before this; and a poster-scale "story" variant was built+REVERTED 2026-07-14 as AI-made. Lesson retained: scale theatrics exceed this brand's ceiling.)
- Tailwind v4 gotcha (learned here): `translate-y-*` compiles to the CSS `translate` PROPERTY, not a `transform` matrix. To animate a mask-rise you MUST transition `[translate]` (or use `transition-transform` which v4 maps to include it) — `transition-[transform]` does NOT cover it and the rise will jump. Also measure it via getComputedStyle().translate, not .transform.
- Scroll-reveal arming: do NOT gate arming on the block's mount position — hero images load after first paint and shift layout, making the position unreliable. Arm unconditionally (when motion allowed) in a pre-paint layout effect; let the IntersectionObserver alone decide WHEN to reveal.
- Hero entrance: one staggered rise on first paint (`entrance-1..5`, 400ms, 80ms steps, cubic-bezier(0.2,0.7,0.2,1)), CSS-only, establishes reading order through timing. Used once per page — the load moment belongs to the hero the way the scroll moment belongs to Act II.
- All motion `motion-safe` gated. Nothing loops, nothing autoplays.
